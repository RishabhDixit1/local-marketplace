# ServiQ — 90-Day Execution Plan

**Theme:** Prove the loop, get revenue, don't build what nobody's used yet.

---

## North Star (Day 90 target)
> **10 completed paid transactions** between strangers on the platform. **5 providers paying for a subscription.** 1 repeat consumer.

---

## Phase 1: Zero to One (Days 1–30)
**Goal:** Get the first transaction. Any transaction.

### Week 1: Strip & Ship
- [ ] Audit the consumer → provider flow end-to-end. Post request → provider matches → chat → quote → pay → complete. Fix anything broken. Kill any page/feature not on this path.
- [ ] Remove AI Launchpad, Lead OS, Trust Graph, Team Workspaces from navigation. They stay in code, invisible.
- [ ] Manual "provider matching" mode: if the auto-match fails, an admin (you) manually assigns the request. No leads lost to tech.
- [ ] Test a mock transaction yourself. Then with a friend. Then with a stranger.

### Week 2-3: Onboard 10 Providers Personally
- [ ] Go to local markets in Noida (Sector 18, Atta Market, GIP周边). Talk to electricians, plumbers, AC repair shops.
- [ ] Sign them up yourself. Take photos of their work. Write their descriptions. Set their prices.
- [ ] No subscription fee for first month. Free trial. Promise them "I'll bring you customers."
- [ ] Give them a WhatsApp number they can reach you on. You are the concierge.

### Week 4: Get the First Paid Transaction
- [ ] Post a real help request yourself (or ask a friend) on the platform.
- [ ] Walk a provider through accepting, quoting, getting paid.
- [ ] Confirm the provider receives payout (Razorpay test).
- [ ] Take screenshots. This is your proof-of-life for investors.

### Week 4: First Provider Subscription
- [ ] After delivering 1-2 transactions, ask one provider to pay ₹299/month.
- [ ] If they say no, ask why. Document every objection. This is your product feedback.

**Phase 1 success metric:** 1 completed transaction + 1 subscription sign-up.

---

## Phase 2: Iterate & Learn (Days 31–60)
**Goal:** Understand the unit economics from real data, not a spreadsheet.

### Provider Pull
- [ ] From the 10 providers, who actually used the app? Who responded to notifications? Who complained about the UI?
- [ ] Fix the top 3 friction points they mention. Don't build anything they didn't ask for.
- [ ] Ask each for 1 referral ("Do you know another electrician who wants more customers?"). Offer them a free week for each referral.

### Consumer Pull
- [ ] Run a hyperlocal experiment: 50 leaflets in Sector 44, 50 in Crossings Republik. "Need a plumber? Download ServiQ." Track how many install.
- [ ] OR: WhatsApp forward to 3 local society groups. "My friend just started a platform — got my AC fixed in 2 hours. Try it." Track installs.
- [ ] OR: Stand outside a hardware store. Offer ₹50 off first service. Collect phone numbers.
- [ ] Pick ONE channel. Do it 10 times. Measure cost per install, cost per transaction.

### Pricing Check
- [ ] At this point you have real data: what did the provider actually charge? What did the consumer actually pay?
- [ ] Is 12% commission painful for the provider? Would they prefer ₹299/month flat fee instead?
- [ ] Try both models with different providers. See which gets more adoption.

**Phase 2 success metric:** 5 completed transactions. Clear CAC from ONE channel. Provider feedback documented.

---

## Phase 3: Decide (Days 61–90)
**Goal:** Make the call — double down, pivot, or raise.

### Unit Economics Reality Check
- [ ] Calculate real CAC (from the channel that worked).
- [ ] Calculate real LTV (how many times did each consumer transact?).
- [ ] Calculate real provider retention (did they stay after the free trial?).
- [ ] If numbers look better than the financial model → you have a business. Build a pitch with real data.
- [ ] If numbers look worse → fix the broken assumption. Is it price? Trust? Not enough providers? Not enough consumers?

### Fundraise Prep
- [ ] If metrics are promising: update the financial model with real data. Prepare pitch deck v2. Target angel investors / accelerate with your ₹10 Cr round narrative.
- [ ] If metrics are weak: don't raise. Extend the runway, keep doing things that don't scale, prove more.

### Build Decision
- [ ] Based on learning: do providers need the mobile app? Do consumers need better search? Is the quote flow too complex?
- [ ] Only now should you build anything new. Everything before this is data collection.

**Phase 3 success metric:** Clear yes/no on whether the business works in Noida.

---

## What NOT to do (Days 1–90)

| Don't Build / Do | Why |
|---|---|
| AI Launchpad enhancements | Zero people have used it. No data to improve from. |
| Lead OS | You don't have lead volume to need an OS. |
| Trust Graph | 10 providers don't need algorithmic trust scoring. |
| Team Workspaces | You're a team of 1-2. Workspaces are irrelevant. |
| Flutter parity features | If web works, ship on web. Mobile can wait. |
| Premium features | Nobody's on the free tier yet. Premium is imaginary. |
| Investor meetings (pre-revenue) | Without real data, you'll waste meetings convincing skeptics. Wait until you have a transaction screenshot. |

---

## Weekly Cadence

**Monday:** Plan the week's 3 most important tasks. Only 3.
**Friday:** Review: Did we get closer to a transaction? What did we learn? Kill anything that didn't help.

**Questions to ask every week:**
1. Did a stranger use our product?
2. Did we make money?
3. Did we talk to a user (not a friend)?

If the answer to all three is "no" for two weeks straight, change what you're doing.

---

## Final Thought

> "Do things that don't scale." — Paul Graham

Your competitive advantage right now isn't the code. It's that you can walk to a market, talk to a plumber, hand-hold a transaction, and learn faster than Urban Company ever could. Move accordingly.
